Research

My research lies at the intersection of natural language processing, health informatics, and responsible AI. I focus on automated detection of stigmatizing language in clinical notes, multi-stage transfer learning, and fairness-aware evaluation across demographic groups.

Automated Detection of Stigmatizing Language in EHRs

Developing multi-stage transfer learning pipelines to identify stigmatizing language in electronic health records, integrating semantic, clinical, and task-specific adaptation to capture subtle tone and context across long clinical narratives.

Stigmatizing Language Toward Gender-Expansive Patients

Building annotated datasets and models to characterize how Gender-expansive patients are described in clinical documentation, including misgendering, descriptor-level stigma, and demographic differences in model performance.

Responsible & Fair Clinical NLP

Evaluating NLP models across demographic subgroups, using odds ratios, subgroup performance metrics, and multi-objective optimization to balance overall accuracy and fairness.
